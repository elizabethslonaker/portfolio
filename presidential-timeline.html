2020 Presidential Election Timeline

A visual representation of the presidential election timeline and process, published in The Shorthorn News.

View Live Site

Overview

In 2020, thousands of college students will cast their first election vote. As a web developer for The Shorthorn, a student run news organization, I saw this as an opportunity to help demystify the election process for college students using visuals. The information is focused on Texas.

The timeframe was 2 weeks. The deadline for publication was on February 18, 2020 - the start of early voting in the Texas Primary.

Target Audience

UTA college students ages 18-24 with a focus on first time voters

Sketch & Iterations

My first ideas focused on using a tree diagram. The idea was to map all the elections pertinent to a UTA student, divided into national, state, and local elections. The problem was that the relationship between these elections was unclear. Instead, I decided to specifically focus on the presidential election dates.



The second idea was to use a dendrogram to illustrate the nation's path to elect a new president in 2020. I wanted to map every step from the primaries to the general election to inaguration. Although the dendrogram proved to not be mobile friendly, this concept became the driving idea moving forward.



Through several iterations, I settled on a basic timeline to map all the key dates from the March Primary to the 2021 January inauguration.

I used 5 icons to visually represent the 5 different stages in the presidential selection process. These icons act as markers for each section of the process, as the user scrolls down the timeline.
